# Why a Black Box for Computers?

*The argument, in ten steps. This is the written version of our [carousel](assets/alcyone-blackbox-carousel.pdf) — the same case, in plain text so anyone (and any machine) can read it without opening a slide deck.*

> Security is not just prevention. Security is accountability.
> **Trust is good. Proof is better.**

---

### 1 · A question worth sitting with

Aviation has a black box. So do trains, ships, power grids, even hospitals. Your computer has
nothing.

**Air. Rail. Sea. Power. Care. — Your PC.** Every critical system in the world learned to keep a
recorder. The one device that runs your money, your work, and your private life did not.

### 2 · The pattern

Every high-stakes field learned the same lesson. When something goes wrong, you cannot rely on
memory, on trust, or on whoever was in the room. You need a record of what actually happened —
one that survives the event and cannot be quietly rewritten.

### 3 · The gap

Now think about the computer. It holds your money, your work, your private life, the systems a
whole company runs on. Yet when something goes wrong on it, we are left to guess. We
reconstruct. We assume. We rarely know.

### 4 · The blind spot

We locked every door but the one in the room. We have gotten good at stopping threats that come
over the network — **network threats, watched.** But the moment a person has the machine in their
hands, that whole defense goes quiet — **the hand on the machine, unseen.**

### 5 · The part nobody guards

The most dangerous person rarely breaks in. They are handed the keys. The new hire. The
contractor. The technician. Legitimate access, doing something it should not, looking exactly
like normal work.

### 6 · Why it goes unseen

Antivirus looks for malware. It was never built to notice a person. It cannot tell the difference
between routine work and a quiet betrayal, because both use a valid login. The tools we trust are
watching the wrong door.

### 7 · So we built one

The black box, for the computer. When a device leaves your control, it records what happens in a
form that cannot be quietly altered. If something goes wrong, the truth is still there.

### 8 · What it actually is

Not prevention. **Evidence.** A black box does not stop the incident — it makes the truth
recoverable afterward. It does not promise nothing will go wrong. It promises that if it does,
you will know exactly what happened, and you will be able to prove it.

### 9 · What we believe

Security has always meant prevention. We think it also means accountability. Trust is good. Proof
is better.

### 10 · Over to you

Every other critical system gets a flight recorder. Should the devices we run our work and our
lives on be any different?

If you work in security, we would genuinely like to hear where you land on this. The disagreement
is the interesting part.
